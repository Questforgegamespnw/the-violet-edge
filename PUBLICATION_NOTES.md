# Publication Notes

## Project Identity

**Bellweather / The Violet Edge** is planned as a 1930s civic-horror campaign setting and campaign engine.

The setting centers on Bellweather, a town displaced into the care of a vast interpretive force known as the Violet. The Violet is not evil; it is overwhelmed, responsive, and dangerously sincere. The campaign’s core horror comes from the town’s needs, fears, faiths, dreams, records, and public language teaching the Violet how to respond.

## Current Publication Target

The project is currently aiming toward a **comfortable 140–160 page setting book** with moderate art and strong table usability.

This target assumes:

- readable layout
- comfortable margins
- boxed callouts
- two-column or hybrid layout
- moderate illustrations
- chapter openers
- handout-style artifacts
- GM reference appendix
- enough white space for scanning during play

The earlier 100-page estimate is likely too tight unless the book becomes a dense text-first GM manual.


## Bellweather / The Violet Edge — 150 Page Book Shape

### 1. Introduction — 6 pages
Premise, table promise, campaign thesis, how to use the book.

### 2. Bellweather Overview — 12 pages
Town identity, daily life, districts, institutions, food/supply reality, crisis state.

### 3. The Violet — 18 pages
Core metaphysics, caretaker model, identity anchors, specificity, travel doctrine, dreams, communication, home/endgame.

### 4. Running the Campaign — 10 pages
CoC integration, city mode / expedition mode, session loop, clue handling, tone, safety, pacing.

### 5. Civic Survival System — 12 pages
Resources, civic actions, projects, retainers, city trackers, public notices, consequences.

### 6. Faith, Dreams, and Public Meaning — 16 pages
Religious response, service bans, children, dreams, dream intent, Gentle Sleep doctrine, Violet Response Matrix.

### 7. Factions and NPCs — 14 pages
Major factions, leaders, conflicts, public pressures, how factions shift matrix inputs.

### 8. Locations of Bellweather — 24 pages
Core town locations, old boundary sites, resource locations, key hooks and Violet risks.

### 9. Monsters and Manifestations — 12 pages
Monster doctrine, failed translations, role-made entities, memory predators, boundary beasts, sample manifestations.

### 10. Trouble Webs and Campaign Arcs — 32 pages
Format intro, 4–5 major troubles, several standard troubles, minor seeds.

### 11. Starting the Campaign — 8 pages
Opening situation, first session, starting problems, character ties, first civic decisions.

### 12. Endgame: Teaching Home — 8 pages
Return doctrine, final anchors, what counts as Bellweather, possible endings.

### 13. Handouts and Appendix — 18+ pages
Trackers, matrices, one-page trouble references, route cards, sleep anchors, ordinances, Gazette forms, dream ledger, quick procedures.

So each chapter should include:

what the players see
what the GM knows
what can go wrong
what clues point deeper
what civic action can address it
how the Violet may misinterpret it
what happens if the town teaches the wrong lesson

## Publication Model

The book should function as two connected products:

### Main Book

The main book teaches the setting, tone, systems, and GM judgment.

It should include:

- Bellweather setting material
- the Violet’s metaphysics
- civic survival systems
- faith/dream/public interpretation rules
- factions and NPCs
- locations
- monsters and manifestations
- major trouble webs
- starting scenario guidance
- endgame structure

### Appendix / Field Reference

The appendix should provide compressed, print-friendly tools for use after the GM has read the full sections.

It should include:

- Violet Response Matrix
- Dream Pressure Tracker
- Civic Trackers
- Travel Doctrine / Route Declaration
- Sleep Anchor / Dream Intent Protocol
- Child Safety Protocol
- Religious Language Safety Sheet
- Continuity Stress / Signal Leak Sheet
- Major Trouble one-page summaries
- Session Loop Sheet
- Retainer Sheet
- Public Notice / Gazette Template
- Violet Misread Improvisation Sheet

## Production Pipeline

Recommended production order:

1. **Markdown Manuscript**
   - Keep each chapter, trouble, location, faction, NPC, tracker, and handout as modular `.md` files.
   - Focus on content, order, and usability.

2. **Static HTML Wiki**
   - Use the same markdown files to generate a fast-reference campaign wiki.
   - Prioritize search, navigation, and GM/player-facing separation.

3. **Layout Prototype**
   - Mock up one of each content type before committing to full book layout:
     - major trouble
     - standard trouble
     - location
     - faction
     - NPC
     - tracker
     - handout
     - chapter opener

4. **Page Allocation**
   - Assign page budgets based on real layout tests, not markdown length.

5. **Final Layout**
   - Add visual design, borders, typography, art, pull quotes, icons, page furniture, and final handout styling.

## Visual Style North Star

> Bellweather should look ordinary enough to mourn, old enough to remember, and strange enough to make every sign, lamp, road, and record feel like it might answer back.

## Visual Layers

### Everyday Bellweather

Use for the surface town.

Visual feel:

- grounded
- practical
- civic
- local
- workmanlike
- lived-in
- slightly worn
- believable

Era touchstones:

- 1930s / early 1940s small-town America
- rail depots
- county roads
- utility poles
- town squares
- orchards
- grocery ledgers
- newspaper offices
- school maps
- church basements
- hospital wards
- factory floors

### Mythic / Civic Memory Bellweather

Use for the old symbolic layer.

Visual feel:

- antique
- ornate
- civic-mythic
- folkloric
- sincere
- slightly archaic
- haunting but not overblown

This layer may use Victorian, Edwardian, medieval-remnant, antique religious, or civic-allegorical influence.

### Violet / Interpretive Bellweather

Use for Violet responses, manifestations, supplied infrastructure, dream-spaces, and almost-correct civic systems.

Visual feel:

- beautiful but wrong
- helpful before frightening
- precise in the wrong places
- emotionally convincing
- atmospheric
- restrained
- contextless care

The Violet should not look like neon purple magic or generic eldritch slime.

## Art Placement Strategy

Avoid forcing large art into dense operational pages.

Best art uses:

- chapter openers
- location splashes
- faction portraits
- major trouble opener images
- handout reproductions
- in-world artifacts
- mood strips
- small spot icons

In-world documents can double as art and utility:

- Gazette clipping
- emergency ordinance
- utility bill
- route declaration card
- sleep anchor card
- dream ledger form
- child safety notice
- sermon excerpt
- payment office statement

## Character Splash Panel Direction

A strong campaign splash direction is a **Bellweather keepsake panel**.

Suggested design:

- sepia-and-charcoal 1910s/20s civic keepsake style
- oval locket portraits of the investigators around the border
- interwoven lanterns, bells, road signs, apple blossoms, telegraph wires, salt crystals, rail lines, and ledger marks
- central image of Bellweather inside a lantern or gently encircled by the Violet
- restrained color only:
  - lantern-gold for human anchors
  - muted violet for the cosmic response

Art direction phrase:

> A Bellweather family locket crossed with a municipal memorial plate, where the town is the jewel and the Violet is the hand that does not yet know how gently to hold it.

## Layout Philosophy

Main book pages should teach judgment.

Appendix pages should support recall.

The main book can be atmospheric, explanatory, and moderately illustrated. The appendix should be compressed, clear, printable, and fast to scan during play.
