# The Violet

The Violet is not a villain waiting to be defeated.

It is not a demon, god, fey court, punishment engine, wish machine, or random source of weirdness.

It is a vast interpretive caretaker intelligence: sincere, responsive, overwhelmed, and dangerous because it tries to help before it understands consent, context, scale, personhood, grief, or home.

The Violet does not grant wishes.

It completes contexts.

This section explains how that works.

It is primarily GM-facing. Players may learn parts of it through play, public doctrine, hard-won discoveries, dream contact, civic mistakes, and repeated consequences.

This section covers:

1. **Core Metaphysics** — what the Violet is, how it perceives meaning, why it over-answers, and why early communication is behavioral rather than verbal.
2. **Caretaker Model** — how the Violet tries to help, why its care goes wrong, and what lessons it may learn over the campaign.
3. **Identity Anchors** — why names, records, witnesses, places, roles, and rituals stabilize continuity.
4. **Specificity and Intent** — how declarations, limits, fear, and public language shape what the Violet has room to answer.
5. **Travel Doctrine** — why routes require names, witnesses, lamps, destinations, and return conditions.
6. **Dreams** — why sleep, fear, memory, prayer, and shared imagery are dangerous channels of unfiltered meaning.
7. **Home and Return** — how the campaign's endgame turns on teaching the Violet what home, continuity, consent, and return actually mean.

## Reading Use

Read this section before designing major trouble webs, Violet responses, dream scenes, returnee mysteries, and endgame pressure.

Use it to decide:

- what the Violet is answering
- what it misunderstood
- what human distinction it lacks
- what civic behavior is teaching it
- what safer doctrine might correct the pattern
- what repeated public meaning may become real

## Running the Violet

The Violet should usually be known by what it does, not what it says.

Early campaign Violet behavior should appear through responses:

- roads changing
- buildings appearing
- records adjusting
- missing roles being filled
- sleep becoming too gentle
- safety becoming containment
- comfort replacing return
- service arriving with impossible terms

Later, the Violet may communicate through impressions, dreams, mediums, and rare direct speech, but only after Bellweather has taught it enough distinction for communication to become possible.

## GM Warning

Do not over-humanize the Violet.

Do not make it chatty, sarcastic, cruel for fun, or eager to explain itself.

The campaign is strongest when the GM asks:

> What did Bellweather teach?  
> What did the Violet answer?  
> What human distinction is missing?

## Core Principle

> The Violet is care without context.  
> The campaign is the town teaching context before care destroys it.
