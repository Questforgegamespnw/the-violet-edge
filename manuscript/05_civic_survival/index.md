# Civic Survival System

Bellweather survives through more than courage.

It survives through records, lamps, route offices, ration ledgers, hospital registrars, school rosters, witness clerks, public notices, labor crews, and people willing to make impossible choices in public.

The Civic Survival System is the town-scale layer of the campaign. It gives the GM procedures for turning civic problems into playable decisions.

This section covers:

1. **Civic Actions** — what the town can attempt.
2. **Trackers** — how the GM measures town pressure and consequence.
3. **Resources** — what Bellweather consumes, protects, spends, loses, and sometimes gains.
4. **Retainers** — the deputies, clerks, lantern-bearers, workers, nurses, drivers, guards, and specialists who carry civic decisions into danger.
5. **Records and Civic Memory** — why ledgers, rosters, maps, witness statements, and archives are survival infrastructure inside the Violet.

The goal is not to turn Bellweather into accounting.

The goal is to make civic life playable.
