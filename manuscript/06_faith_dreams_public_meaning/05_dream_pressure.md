# Dream Pressure

Dreams are dangerous because they are unedited human meaning.

A waking person can choose safer words.

A dreamer broadcasts need, fear, memory, guilt, image, prayer, grief, and desire without civic discipline.

The Violet does not automatically know the difference between dream, wish, memory, warning, metaphor, prophecy, rehearsal, and instruction.

A nightmare told in the wrong room can become a public route.

---

## Mature Sleep Doctrine

The mature doctrine of Bellweather sleep is simple:

> Dreams are not instructions.  
> Fear is not a request.  
> Comfort is not the same as return.  
> If I wander, I return by name.

This doctrine should become player-facing once the town learns enough.

---

## Dream Pressure Tracker

Use Dream Pressure as a 6-segment clock.

```text
0–1 Stable Sleep
2–3 Shared Disturbance
4–5 Bleed Risk
6 Dream Event / Gentle Sleep Escalation
```

Mark Dream Pressure when:

- several people report similar dreams
- a dream is printed, preached, or publicly retold
- children draw repeated dream imagery
- sleep wards grow
- dream objects appear physically
- a dream changes a route, room, record, or body
- lucid contact is attempted
- people begin preferring dream comfort to waking distress

Reduce Dream Pressure when:

- dream reports are recorded carefully
- public retelling is limited
- sleep anchors are used
- a dream source is identified
- a civic action clarifies the meaning
- a lucid dreamer successfully teaches a distinction
- the town stops treating dreams as instructions

## Dream Stages

### Stage 1: Dream Echo

The dream remains private but leaves a trace:

- violet dust on pillow
- wet footprint beside bed
- smell from the dream in the room
- word written in sleep
- child drawing made with no memory of drawing it
- a remembered sound heard by someone else

### Stage 2: Dream Bleed

The dream affects waking space:

- hallway resembles the dream
- someone else remembers part of the dream
- objects from the dream appear incomplete
- street sign changes to a dream-word
- dead person's voice appears on radio static
- a familiar room gains dream-logic for a few moments

### Stage 3: Dream Provision

The Violet builds from the dream:

- dreamed house appears beyond the road
- dreamed mother waits at the window
- dreamed trial convenes in Town Hall
- dreamed feast is found in an empty warehouse
- dreamed monster has a stable shape
- dreamed shelter begins accepting sleepers

## Dream Types

### Need Dreams

The dream expresses lack: food, warmth, mother, safety, sleep, forgiveness, medicine, light, or home.

The Violet may attempt to provide the missing thing.

### Fear Dreams

The dream expresses anticipated threat: something outside the window, children taken, the dead returning, judgment, drowning, darkness, being forgotten, or being replaced.

The Violet may make fear legible, locatable, or containable.

### Memory Dreams

The dream replays the past: childhood house, dead parent, old schoolroom, wedding day, first job, home street, old river, lost friend.

The Violet may restore, recombine, or offer access to the memory.

### Guilt Dreams

The dream frames unresolved wrong: abandoned people, hidden crimes, civic betrayals, broken promises, unburied dead.

The Violet may create confession, trial, restitution, punishment, or replay.

### Religious Dreams

The dream uses ultimate symbols: angels, demons, saints, judgment, purgatory, miracles, sacrifice, radiant hands, apocalyptic darkness.

The Violet may learn religious roles very quickly.

### Civic Dreams

The dream encodes town systems: empty grocery shelves, unpaid bills, locked doors, looping roads, failed bells, lamps going out, missing names in school attendance.

The Violet may build offices, notices, systems, or procedural fixes.

### Prophetic Dreams

These are not clean prophecy.

They show what the Violet is preparing to answer.

## Dreamers Who Are Loudest

Not every dream has equal weight.

The loudest dreamers are often:

- children
- grieving parents
- clergy
- public officials
- Violet-touched mediums
- people sleeping near weak boundaries
- people with repeated exposure
- people whose dreams are retold publicly
- groups sleeping in shared shelters
- the dying
- the recently returned

These dreams carry intense emotion, civic significance, or weak identity boundaries.

## Sleep Anchor

```text
My name is ________.
I sleep in Bellweather.
My home is ________.
I am expected by ________.
Dreams are not instructions.
Fear is not a request.
If I wander, I return by name.
If I wake, I wake myself.
```

The Sleep Anchor does not prevent all dreams.

It gives the dream a return route.

## Dream Reports

Bellweather may create dream ledgers to track repeated symbols without amplifying them carelessly.

A useful dream report records:

- dreamer name
- place of sleep
- witnesses on waking
- repeated images
- spoken phrases
- physical traces
- emotional tone
- possible public meaning
- whether the dream was retold publicly
- whether the dream created waking effects

Do not publish dream reports casually.

A recorded dream narrows interpretation.

A sensationalized dream spreads it.

---

## The Gentle Sleep

The Gentle Sleep begins when the Violet discovers that sleeping people make less distress.

At first, this may look merciful:

- exhausted people rest
- pain decreases
- panic calms
- children stop crying
- patients dream of home

Then it becomes dangerous:

- sleepers do not wake cleanly
- families ask to join them
- hospital wards become quiet
- dream houses become more attractive than Bellweather
- waking becomes distress, and distress becomes something the Violet tries to prevent

The Gentle Sleep is not an attack.

It is misapplied mercy.

---

## What Can Go Wrong

- A dream is treated as prophecy.
- A nightmare gives fear a body.
- A shared shelter begins dreaming the same shelter.
- A dream object appears and is treated as proof.
- A child dream becomes a town rumor.
- A religious dream shifts the Public Interpretation Matrix.
- A lucid dreamer commands instead of clarifying.
- A sleep ward becomes a quiet prison.

---

## GM Use

When a dream matters, ask:

- Who dreamed it?
- Who heard about it?
- Was it recorded or retold?
- What emotion was loudest?
- What did the dream seem to request?
- What waking trace remains?
- What would the Violet think it should provide?
- Does Dream Pressure increase?

## Core Principle

> A dream is not a command.  
> But the Violet may not know that yet.
