# Faith, Dreams, and Public Meaning

Bellweather survives inside a reality that listens to meaning.

That makes faith, public language, dreams, children, sermons, newspapers, rumor, grief, and shared symbols mechanically important.

This section explains how Bellweather teaches the Violet without always meaning to.

It covers:

1. **Religion and the Violet** — how faith, ritual, and theology give the unknown dangerous vocabulary.
2. **Public Interpretation Matrix** — how the GM tracks what the town believes and how the Violet answers.
3. **Service Bans** — how Bellweather may restrict mass religious speech without simply declaring faith the enemy.
4. **Children and the Violet** — why children are bright, unstable signals and how to protect them without using them as shock props.
5. **Dream Pressure** — how dreams move from private fear to public infrastructure.
6. **Lucid Dreaming** — how disciplined dream contact can investigate, clarify, or teach the Violet.

The goal is not to punish prayer, dreams, imagination, or public feeling.

The goal is to make shared meaning playable.

## Safety First

This section touches real-world faith, children in danger, grief, dreams, identity, and religious horror. Before using this material heavily, review `01_introduction/04_content_and_safety_notes.md`.

The campaign works whether the table uses full real-world faiths, soft real-world faiths, fictionalized faiths, or secular public meaning.

## Core Principle

> Words are infrastructure.  
> Dreams are routes.  
> Public meaning teaches the Violet what kind of answer to become.
