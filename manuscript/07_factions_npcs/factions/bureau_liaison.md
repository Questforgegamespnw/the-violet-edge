# Bureau Liaison

The Bureau Liaison is optional.

Use this faction if the campaign wants outside authority, containment logic, classified records, federal pressure, occult intelligence, or anomaly-management horror.

The Liaison may be physically trapped in Bellweather, communicating from the outside, or represented by pre-crisis files and agents already embedded in town.

## Public Role

Publicly, the Bureau offers expertise, discipline, secrecy, and a larger frame.

Privately, it may see Bellweather less as a town and more as an incident.

## Private Pressure

The Bureau may know enough to be useful and enough to be dangerous.

It may want to save Bellweather. It may want to seal Bellweather. It may want to study Bellweather. It may want the town to survive only as long as the anomaly can be understood.

## What They Want

- containment
- classification
- controlled information
- evidence collection
- suppression of panic
- prevention of external spread
- access to Violet responses
- authority over anomalous persons, sites, and documents

## What They Can Provide

- technical instruments
- prior files
- outside terminology
- disciplined procedures
- quarantine methods
- field agents
- communications equipment
- occult or scientific context

## What They May Demand

- secrecy
- custody of returned persons
- control of records
- restricted movement
- authority over expeditions
- confiscation of anomalous objects
- silence about Bureau involvement

## What Can Go Wrong

- the Bureau reduces people to cases
- containment teaches the Violet that safety means isolation
- outside terminology becomes dangerous public language
- agents classify a person as an event
- Town Hall loses legitimacy to unelected authority
- players must choose between civic truth and operational secrecy

## Public Interpretation Pressure

The Bureau often shifts toward:

- Utility / Service
- System-Shaping
- Boundary-Shaping
- Judge / Trial

If badly handled, it can become the Kindly Bureau's human mirror.

## City Mode Hooks

- A Bureau file predicts the service ban before it happens.
- An agent demands custody of a returned child.
- Bureau equipment identifies a “false” person everyone loves.
- The Committee must decide whether to cooperate with outside authority.
- A classified phrase turns out to be safer than local religious language, but morally colder.

## Location Ties

Possible locations: Town Hall basement office, rail office records room, sealed county archive, Bureau field car, quarantine shed, radio room.

## Core Tension

> The Bureau can help Bellweather survive by treating it like an incident. Bellweather needs to remain a town.
