# Emergency Civic Committee

The Emergency Civic Committee is Bellweather's official center of crisis authority.

It may be the old town council under a new name, a hastily assembled coalition, or a formal emergency government made from the mayor's office, deputies, hospital leadership, clergy, union representatives, clerks, and practical citizens.

Its job is simple to say and almost impossible to do:

> Keep Bellweather alive without teaching the Violet that survival means control.

## Public Role

The Committee issues orders, authorizes expeditions, controls public notices, coordinates rationing, manages curfews, assigns labor, and attempts to keep the town from splintering.

To most residents, it is either:

- the last recognizable authority left
- a group of frightened people pretending procedure can save them
- a necessary evil
- the only thing keeping the panic organized

## Private Pressure

The Committee knows its legitimacy is fragile.

Every order it issues can become public doctrine. Every public doctrine can become Violet input.

It is not only deciding what the town does. It is deciding what the town sounds like while afraid.

## What They Want

- maintain public order
- ration supplies without revolt
- keep Eastgate procedures disciplined
- prevent dangerous headlines, sermons, and rumors
- preserve records
- authorize expeditions without losing too many people
- keep factions inside the civic process
- prove Bellweather is still a town, not a mob

## What They Can Provide

- legal authority
- public notice boards
- route permits
- ration cards
- emergency labor assignments
- meeting space
- witness clerks
- access to municipal records
- pressure on other institutions
- legitimacy for risky civic actions

## What They May Demand

- curfew compliance
- service bans or assembly restrictions
- ration registration
- quarantine cooperation
- surrender of private stores
- censorship or headline review
- expedition reporting
- loyalty to Committee decisions

## What Can Go Wrong

- order becomes control
- records become more important than people
- public safety becomes an excuse to abandon the outskirts
- the Committee bans unsafe words but fails to offer safer language
- factions begin treating Committee approval as the only proof of personhood
- the Violet learns that a calm town is a successful town

## Public Interpretation Pressure

The Committee most often shifts the Matrix toward:

- Utility / Service
- Judge / Trial
- Guardian / Mercy
- System-Shaping
- Shelter-Shaping

At its best, it can also support Unknown / Unnamed by refusing premature labels and teaching the town to clarify before naming.

## City Mode Hooks

- A crowd demands access to a closed Committee meeting.
- A public notice is technically safe but morally cruel.
- The Committee must decide whether to quarantine returned people.
- A faction demands a seat on the Committee.
- A clerk discovers that a Committee order has been rewritten overnight.
- The council must choose whether to spend Public Trust enforcing an unpopular policy.

## Location Ties

Primary location: Town Hall Square.

Secondary ties: Gazette Office, Eastgate, Mercy Hospital, schoolhouse, ration lines, church basements.

These should be sharpened during the Section 8 location pass.

## Core Tension

> Bellweather needs authority. The Violet may mistake authority for care.
