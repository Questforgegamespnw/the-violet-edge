# Faith Coalition

The Faith Coalition is Bellweather's religious and moral pressure made visible.

It includes clergy, congregants, shelter volunteers, funeral workers, devotional societies, household faith networks, and public speakers trying to give language to an event no one can safely name.

## Public Role

The Coalition comforts the frightened, names the dead, hosts shelters, organizes food and care, holds funerals, and gives people a moral framework for suffering.

That makes it powerful.

It also makes it dangerous.

## Private Pressure

The Coalition is divided.

Some believe the Violet is demonic. Some call it judgment. Some see mercy. Some suspect purgatory, old compact, angel, false god, or an unknown thing that must not be named too soon.

Every sermon risks becoming infrastructure.

## What They Want

- protect the right to pray, mourn, gather, and bury the dead
- prevent despair
- explain what has happened without feeding panic
- keep children and families spiritually cared for
- resist Town Hall overreach
- avoid giving the Violet a dangerous role

## What They Can Provide

- shelters
- grief care
- public trust
- funeral records
- volunteers
- moral authority
- calming language
- safe sermon doctrine
- resistance to cult formation
- insight into public fear

## What They May Demand

- exemption from service bans
- space for funerary rites
- involvement in public language review
- protection for congregants
- recognition that faith is not the enemy
- permission to conduct controlled services

## What Can Go Wrong

- a sermon names the Violet as demon, god, judge, or savior
- underground services amplify fear
- a service ban fuels cult recruitment
- grief becomes mass request
- public prayer asks for comfort without consent
- gratitude for a miracle becomes doctrine

## Public Interpretation Pressure

The Coalition can shift nearly any interpretation axis, especially:

- Demon / Enemy
- Judge / Trial
- Guardian / Mercy
- Purgatory / Passage
- False God / Worship
- Unknown / Unnamed

Its safest role is helping the town mourn, clarify, and refuse premature naming.

## City Mode Hooks

- The Committee proposes Emergency Ordinance 17.
- A preacher refuses to stop calling the Violet a demon.
- Sister Agnes argues for mercy language without worship.
- Families demand a mass funeral for the missing.
- A prayer meeting appears to have been answered.
- The Society of the Answered recruits from people the Coalition failed to comfort.

## Location Ties

Primary ties: Saint Orra's Church, Revival Hall, synagogue / benevolent society, prayer room, shelters, cemetery, hospital chapel.

## Core Tension

> Faith can keep Bellweather human. Faith can also teach the Violet a name too soon.
