# Lampworkers' Union

The Lampworkers' Union controls one of Bellweather's most important survival systems: light.

Streetlamps, route lanterns, hospital lamps, signal lights, ward housings, glass repair, and emergency lighting all depend on workers who are suddenly being asked to risk their lives for everyone else's clarity.

## Public Role

The Union keeps Bellweather lit.

In ordinary times, it fought over wages, hours, safety, contracts, and pride. In the Violet, those old fights become existential.

A lit route may return.

An unlit one may not.

## Private Pressure

The Union knows that desperate civic leaders may start treating workers as expendable infrastructure.

They are not opposed to saving Bellweather. They are opposed to being spent like oil.

## What They Want

- worker safety protocols
- fair ration priority for dangerous shifts
- no disposable repair crews
- clear expedition authority
- protection for poor districts, not only Town Center
- fuel and glass allocation
- recognition that light work is survival work

## What They Can Provide

- lantern repair
- streetlamp maintenance
- signal lamp production
- route lighting teams
- technical crews
- knowledge of fuel stores
- labor mobilization
- practical skepticism toward magical explanations
- rumors from working districts

## What They May Demand

- ration priority for night crews
- representation on the Emergency Civic Committee
- refusal rights for unsafe routes
- public acknowledgment of worker deaths
- investigation of old symbols beneath the factory floor
- protection for worker housing and families

## What Can Go Wrong

- a strike shuts down route safety
- a rushed repair crew disappears
- poor districts lose light first
- the Violet supplies replacement workers who know their roles but not their lives
- the town begins equating light with obedience
- Jonah Pike or another union figure becomes a rival civic authority

## Public Interpretation Pressure

The Union can push the Matrix toward:

- Utility / Service
- Boundary-Shaping
- System-Shaping
- Old Compact / Reciprocity

If exploited, it may also push toward Judge / Trial: who deserves light, who must work, who owes what.

## City Mode Hooks

- The Union refuses to send another crew without named return protocols.
- Workers find old boundary marks under the Lampworks floor.
- A Violet-supplied lamp burns without fuel and everyone wants to use it.
- Town Hall asks for emergency double shifts.
- A poor ward accuses the council of saving Market Street first.

## Location Ties

Primary location: The Lampworks.

Secondary ties: Eastgate, worker housing, rail yard, hospital generator rooms, route lantern racks.

## Core Tension

> Bellweather needs light. The workers need proof they are not being offered to it.
