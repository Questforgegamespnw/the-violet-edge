# Old Families

The Old Families are not a formal organization, but everyone knows who they are.

They own land, sit on boards, preserve family papers, bury scandals, sponsor charities, influence elections, remember older names, and quietly decide which histories are respectable enough to repeat.

## Public Role

To the public, the Old Families are civic memory, money, property, and obligation.

They fund memorials, hospitals, church repairs, scholarships, and preservation societies. They also control cellars, archives, deeds, old maps, private roads, and quiet favors.

## Private Pressure

Some Old Families know more about Bellweather's boundary history than they admit.

Others know nothing supernatural, but they know how to protect status under crisis.

Both types are dangerous.

## What They Want

- preserve property rights
- protect family records
- avoid public blame for old boundary practices
- maintain influence over rationing, records, and civic legitimacy
- keep certain names, places, or events from becoming public language
- prevent the town from deciding that old wealth equals old guilt

## What They Can Provide

- private archives
- old maps
- family ledgers
- cellar stores
- property access
- funding
- political pressure
- hidden routes
- old keys
- testimony from elders

## What They May Demand

- protection of family homes
- secrecy
- priority for private stores
- control over old records
- suppression of scandalous histories
- recognition that their families built Bellweather

## What Can Go Wrong

- they hide information needed to stabilize a boundary site
- old deeds contradict current maps
- a family attempts to define who “belongs” in Bellweather
- the Violet treats property records as moral truth
- private charity becomes public control
- an old family bargains with the Violet using inherited language

## Public Interpretation Pressure

The Old Families may shift the Matrix toward:

- Old Compact / Reciprocity
- Judge / Trial
- System-Shaping
- Memory-Shaping

If handled well, they can preserve useful anchors. If handled poorly, they can teach the Violet that continuity means hierarchy.

## City Mode Hooks

- A family offers food stores in exchange for record access.
- A deed names a road that does not exist anymore.
- A private archive contains an older map of Eastgate.
- A family matriarch insists the old wall must not be repaired.
- The public learns one family knew the Violet Edge was possible.

## Location Ties

Likely ties: Old Wall, Old Levy, cemetery ridge, private homes, Historical Society, salt mine, old watchtower, Crow Mill.

## Core Tension

> Old memory can anchor Bellweather, but old power may decide what gets remembered.
