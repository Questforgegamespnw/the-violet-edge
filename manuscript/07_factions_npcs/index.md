# Factions and NPCs

Bellweather is not governed by one voice.

Every institution, neighborhood, profession, family network, public office, and frightened congregation is trying to keep the town alive while also protecting its own people, language, history, and authority.

Use this section when the GM needs the town to push back.

Factions are not side quests. They are civic pressure made human.

NPCs are not only clue dispensers. They are people whose choices, fears, reputations, loyalties, and public language can teach the Violet what Bellweather means.

## How to Use This Section

Use factions to create pressure during City Mode:

- who demands a Council Slot
- who opposes a public notice
- who controls a resource
- who is trusted by the public
- who can calm a crowd
- who can make things worse by speaking loudly and sincerely
- who benefits if the town adopts a dangerous interpretation
- who suffers if the council delays

Use NPCs to personalize those pressures:

- the mayor who edits notices because words cause outcomes
- the doctor who wants quarantine before comfort
- the union organizer who refuses disposable labor
- the schoolteacher whose children draw impossible maps
- the child whose drawings, questions, and needs reveal what adult systems miss
- the faith leader who knows the Violet is not simply evil

## Faction File Format

Each faction page includes:

- public role
- private pressure
- what they want
- what they can provide
- what they may demand
- how they shift public interpretation
- what they can teach the Violet
- City Mode hooks
- location ties to expand after Section 8

## NPC File Format

Each NPC page includes:

- public face
- private pressure
- what they want
- what they know or suspect
- what they can offer
- what they may do under stress
- associated factions and locations
- background, appearance, physical impression, family ties, reputation, leadership style, private contradiction, and quick portrayal
- Signal Leak risk
- GM use at the table
