# Miriam Bell

Miriam Bell knows that silence is not neutrality.

If the Gazette does not print something, Market Street will invent it before noon.

## Background

Miriam Bell grew up around ink, proof sheets, civic notices, and arguments about what the public had a right to know. Her family name may be old Bellweather or may simply sound like it should be, which irritates the Old Families more than she admits.

She became the Gazette's central voice by being faster than officials, more careful than rumor, and just stubborn enough to print what powerful people preferred buried. Before the crisis, that made her troublesome. After the crisis, it makes her dangerous in both directions.

## Appearance

Miriam is in her thirties or early forties, ink-stained, sharp-eyed, usually dressed in practical skirts or trousers depending on table tone and era handling. She keeps pencils in her hair, pockets, and behind one ear, then forgets where she put them.

## Physical Impression

She leans toward people when questioning them and away when she already knows they are lying. Her energy is quick, restless, and watchful. She talks with her hands when excited and becomes very still when deciding whether a headline will hurt people.

## Family and Personal Ties

Miriam's closest family may be the Gazette itself: printers, copy boys, route carriers, and old press operators. She may have a younger brother at the Lampworks, an aunt in the River Ward, or a parent whose name still opens doors she refuses to use.

She knows more secrets than most clergy and more lies than most deputies.

## Public Reputation

Half the town accuses her of stirring trouble; the other half brings her trouble because no one else will write it down. People say they do not trust the Gazette, then check the bulletin board three times a day.

## Leadership Style

Miriam leads through questions, public pressure, correction, and refusal to let officials own reality. She values truth, but in Bellweather truth must now be timed, framed, and sometimes withheld until safer words exist.

Her danger is that exposure can become its own righteousness.

## Private Contradiction

Miriam believes silence kills. She is slowly learning that some true words, printed too loudly, can become instructions.

## Quick Portrayal

**First Impression:** A reporter with ink on her fingers and a fuse burning behind her eyes.  
**Voice:** Fast, dry, incisive.  
**Posture:** Leaning in, notebook ready, shoulder against doorframes.  
**Usual Location:** Gazette office, bulletin board, Town Hall steps, Market Street.  
**Known For:** Printing what others mutter.  
**Soft Spot:** Apprentices, whistleblowers, frightened witnesses.  
**Hard Line:** Official lies that erase people.  
**Pressure Trigger:** Being told the public cannot handle the truth.  
**If She Breaks:** She prints the truth without asking what it will teach.

## Public Face

Gazette editor. Sharp. Restless. Hard to intimidate. Always thinking in headlines and corrections.

She wants to publish enough truth to keep the town sane without printing a phrase the Violet will answer.

## Private Pressure

The radio has picked up broadcasts using tomorrow's date.

She has not decided whether that is a story, a warning, or bait.

## What She Wants

- public truth
- rumor correction
- access to Committee decisions
- safe headline doctrine
- protection for reporters
- paper supply
- answers about the radio broadcasts

## What She Knows or Suspects

- official silence breeds worse language
- repeated headlines become town belief
- corrections matter less than first impressions
- the Violet may imitate notice language

## What She Can Offer

- public reach
- archives
- witnesses
- old clippings
- rumor networks
- radio monitoring
- pressure on Town Hall

## Under Stress

Miriam may publish too much too fast, defy review, or treat truth as automatically safer than restraint.

Her worst version teaches the Violet that public revelation matters more than context.

## Signal Leak Risk

> “If people knew the truth, they would choose better.”

## GM Use

Use Miriam when the players need public information to become active pressure. She can be ally, critic, leak, and consequence engine.
