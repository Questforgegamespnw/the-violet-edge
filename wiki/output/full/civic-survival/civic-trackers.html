Trackers

Trackers are not scoreboards.

They are pressure instruments.

They tell the GM what kinds of consequences are becoming more likely, what the town can still rely on, and what Bellweather is teaching the Violet through repeated public behavior.

Use only the trackers that matter right now.

Too many trackers will bury the table.

Two Kinds of Trackers

Bellweather uses two broad tracker types.

Civic Capacity Trackers

These describe what the town can do.

They are rated from 0–5 and may become the base dice pool for City Mode actions.

0 Broken
1 Fragile
2 Strained
3 Functional
4 Strong
5 Exceptional / deeply anchored

Pressure Clocks

These describe danger building toward an event.

They are usually 6-segment clocks.

0–1 Stable
2–3 Strained
4–5 Critical
6 Crisis / event triggers

The same table does not need every tracker active at once.

Pick the few that match the current trouble.

Core Civic Capacity Trackers

Survival

Food, water, medicine, shelter, sanitation, emergency care, and basic bodily continuity.

Use Survival when the town tries to feed people, heal people, shelter people, prevent disease, manage triage, or keep vulnerable residents alive.

Low Survival means hunger, exposure, infection, exhaustion, black markets, and desperate requests become common.

Stability

Law, morale, public order, trust, institutional legitimacy, and the town’s ability to make decisions without tearing itself apart.

Use Stability when the town issues orders, manages panic, negotiates with factions, enforces rationing, regulates assembly, or tries to keep people from turning on one another.

Low Stability means rumors outrun facts, factions gain power, crowds gather, and the Violet receives fear as public instruction.

Foothold

Maps, lamps, gates, route offices, district boundaries, road signs, landmarks, and stable civic geography.

Use Foothold when the town tries to stabilize Eastgate, preserve a route, mark a district, reclaim a boundary site, repair lamp grids, or prevent parts of Bellweather from becoming vague.

Low Foothold means roads drift, maps lie, districts blur, and returning becomes harder.

Understanding

Research, interpretation, pattern recognition, scientific inquiry, occult study, public doctrine, and the town’s ability to tell a useful theory from a dangerous one.

Use Understanding when the town studies the Violet, compares clues, investigates dreams, interprets repeated manifestations, or drafts safer doctrine.

Low Understanding means the town reacts emotionally, confuses coincidence with law, and teaches the Violet through bad conclusions.

Contact

Expeditions, missing-person recovery, outside signals, scouting, diplomacy, and deliberate attempts to reach beyond confirmed Bellweather.

Use Contact when the town sends patrols, attempts radio communication, searches for named missing people, contacts other settlements, or speaks through mediums.

Low Contact means isolation, rumor, false rescue, and dangerous longing.

Adaptation

New protocols, training, technology, rituals, countermeasures, institutional learning, and the town’s willingness to change without losing itself.

Use Adaptation when Bellweather creates route offices, witness training, dream protocols, recognition tests, retainer systems, public notice templates, or new lamp procedures.

Low Adaptation means old habits repeat under new laws of reality.

Return

Continuity, home doctrine, final anchors, civic consent, and the town’s ability to define what returning home must mean.

Use Return when Bellweather works toward the endgame: who counts, what returns, what changes remain, what the town refuses to erase, and what home must include.

Low Return means the town may seek escape without understanding what would be lost.

Special Pressure Trackers

The Plane Learns / Violet Learning

This tracker measures how strongly the Violet has learned from Bellweather’s repeated behavior.

It does not mean the Violet is getting smarter in a human way.

It means certain interpretations are becoming easier for it to repeat.

Track when:

  • public language repeats
  • emergency policies become doctrine
  • worship or fear stabilizes around one interpretation
  • dreams converge
  • a Violet response is accepted as normal
  • the town rewards a dangerous solution
  • a civic action teaches the wrong lesson

High Violet Learning means the same kind of answer will appear more quickly, more confidently, and with less visible uncertainty.

Dream Pressure

Dream Pressure measures how much sleep, shared imagery, private fear, and unconscious meaning are entering public reality.

Track when:

  • multiple people share a dream
  • children repeat dream imagery
  • a dream is printed, preached, or ritualized
  • sleepers do not wake cleanly
  • a dream object appears physically
  • lucid contact is attempted
  • sleep wards grow

High Dream Pressure means the boundary between rest, fear, comfort, and captivity is weakening.

Public Interpretation

Public Interpretation is not one numeric track unless the GM wants it to be.

It is a map of what the town currently believes the Violet is.

Common axes include:

  • Demon / Enemy
  • Judge / Trial
  • Guardian / Mercy
  • Purgatory / Passage
  • Old Compact / Reciprocity
  • False God / Worship
  • Utility / Service
  • Unknown / Unnamed

Track which interpretation is currently loudest, who is spreading it, and what civic action is reinforcing it.

Town Stress

Town Stress is the town’s general emotional overload.

It is useful during Session Loop resolution.

When Town Stress is low, successes are cleaner.

When Town Stress is high, each die showing 1 on a City Mode roll may create a complication even if the action succeeds.

High Town Stress colors the briefing scene: crowds gather, citizens demand answers, ignored problems find a voice, and public language becomes less careful.

Suggested Starting Values

For an early campaign start, use:

  • Survival 3
  • Stability 3
  • Foothold 2
  • Understanding 1
  • Contact 1
  • Adaptation 1
  • Return 0

Then choose two active pressure clocks:

  • Dream Pressure 1
  • Violet Learning 1

And one current Public Interpretation axis, usually:

  • Unknown / Unnamed
  • Judge / Trial
  • Guardian / Mercy
  • Demon / Enemy

Adjust based on the opening situation.

How Trackers Move

Move a tracker when the fiction changes.

Do not move trackers because time passed.

Increase a capacity when:

  • the town solves a problem with repeatable procedure
  • a civic action succeeds cleanly
  • an institution becomes more reliable
  • a faction joins the effort
  • the town learns a useful distinction
  • a route, record, or resource becomes more stable

Decrease a capacity when:

  • a key institution fails
  • public trust breaks
  • an important record is corrupted
  • a route closes
  • a resource crisis spreads
  • a faction withdraws cooperation
  • the Violet learns a dangerous lesson

Fill a pressure clock when:

  • the problem is ignored
  • a public phrase repeats
  • a resource is overused
  • a faction escalates
  • dreams spread
  • a civic action succeeds in a harmful way
  • an expedition returns with consequences

Reduce a pressure clock when:

  • the town addresses the source directly
  • public language is corrected
  • a resource is replenished or rationed fairly
  • witnesses stabilize the issue
  • the GM wants to show real civic success

Using Trackers at the Table

During play, keep active tracking small.

For a normal session, track:

  • one civic capacity
  • one resource clock
  • one pressure clock
  • one public interpretation axis
  • one faction pressure

If the players are focused on Eastgate, track Foothold, Oil/Light, Contact, and the route ledger.

If they are focused on the hospital, track Survival, Medicine, Dream Pressure, and public fear.

If they are focused on sermons, track Stability, Public Interpretation, Dream Pressure, and Violet Learning.

Tracker Consequence Prompts

When a tracker worsens, ask:

  • What gets harder now?
  • Who notices first?
  • Which faction uses this?
  • Which public phrase spreads?
  • What does the Violet now think helps?
  • What civic action could address this?
  • What expedition hook appears?

When a tracker improves, ask:

  • What procedure worked?
  • Who gains trust?
  • What danger becomes less immediate?
  • What does the town now believe it can do?
  • What did Bellweather teach correctly?

Open Calibration Note

The exact number of dots and clock segments can be tuned during playtest.

What matters is not mathematical precision.

What matters is that trackers make civic pressure visible, consequence-driven, and playable.

Core Principle

Trackers do not tell the story for the GM.

They tell the GM where the story is pressing hardest.
