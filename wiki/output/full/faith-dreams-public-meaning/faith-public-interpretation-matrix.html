Public Interpretation Matrix

The Public Interpretation Matrix tracks input.

It asks:

What does Bellweather believe, preach, fear, print, dream, pray, repeat, and reward?

The Response Behavior Matrix tracks output.

It asks:

How has the Violet learned to provide, protect, threaten, question, imitate, systematize, or shape reality?

Together, the two matrices give the GM the Violet's current public-facing behavior.

This is not a mind-control meter.

It is a map of repeated meaning.

When to Update the Matrix

Update the matrix when public meaning changes.

Good triggers include:

  • sermon language spreads
  • the Gazette prints a dangerous headline
  • Town Hall issues a major notice
  • a crowd repeats a phrase
  • a dream is shared by many people
  • a faction names the Violet publicly
  • a rescue, miracle, punishment, or public failure becomes famous
  • a civic action teaches the Violet a repeatable pattern
  • a player chooses safer public language and it catches on

Do not update for every private comment.

Update when meaning becomes public, repeated, emotional, authoritative, ritualized, or useful.

Scoring

Each axis can be scored from 0–5.

0 Absent / not currently shaping events
1 Present as rumor or private interpretation
2 Repeated in limited circles
3 Publicly visible and factionally useful
4 Dominant in major public spaces
5 Deeply taught; the Violet repeats it confidently

Scores should move when the fiction moves.

A single sermon might not raise an axis. A sermon repeated, argued over, quoted in the Gazette, and used to justify policy probably does.

Public Interpretation Matrix

Interpretation Axes

Demon / Enemy

The town believes the Violet is hostile, evil, corrupting, or predatory.

Common evidence:

  • sermons name it devil, demon, tempter, or invader
  • armed patrols describe every manifestation as an enemy
  • children draw horns or monsters because adults keep saying monster
  • the Gazette prints enemy language

Risks:

  • threat-shapes become more common
  • fear gains bodies
  • negotiation becomes harder
  • the Violet learns that Bellweather expects attack

Judge / Trial

The town believes the Violet is testing, punishing, revealing guilt, or demanding confession.

Risks:

  • confession rooms
  • guilt made visible
  • trials without judges
  • punishment notices
  • repeated moral ordeals

Guardian / Mercy

The town believes the Violet protects, shelters, heals, comforts, or spares.

Risks:

  • locked shelters
  • dream custody
  • sedation
  • overprotection
  • captivity-as-safety

Purgatory / Passage

The town believes it is dead, waiting, being sorted, or passing through a spiritual threshold.

Risks:

  • stations
  • waiting rooms
  • ferrymen
  • names called in order
  • dead and living sorted by unclear criteria

Old Compact / Reciprocity

The town believes the Violet obeys bargains, boundaries, offerings, taboos, or old agreements.

Risks:

  • offering logic
  • boundary rules
  • roads that demand payment
  • people trying to negotiate without understanding terms

False God / Worship

The town believes the Violet should be thanked, adored, obeyed, appeased, or petitioned.

Risks:

  • cult formation (See 01_religion_and_the_violet.md for the full cult formation procedure.)
  • miracles becoming doctrine
  • chosen speakers
  • shrines
  • dependency

Utility / Service

The town believes the Violet is a system, utility, service provider, bureau, or civic machine.

Risks:

  • payment offices
  • service ledgers
  • automatic procedures
  • supplied workers
  • compliance without consent

Unknown / Unnamed

The town refuses premature naming and preserves uncertainty while gathering evidence.

Risks:

  • ambiguity remains frightening
  • factions may exploit lack of answers

Benefits:

  • fewer role-locked manifestations
  • more questioning behavior
  • better path toward endgame clarification

Response Behavior Matrix

Dominant Blend Rule

Do not just look at the highest number.

Look at the top two or three interpretation axes and the top two or three behavior axes.

Examples:

  • Demon 4 + Threat-Shaping 5: hostile forms, demonic imagery, fear made visible.
  • Guardian 4 + Shelter-Shaping 5: comforting confinement, locked safety, dream houses.
  • Judge 4 + System-Shaping 4: courts, confession rooms, punishment notices, guilt ledgers.
  • Utility 5 + System-Shaping 5: power stations, payment offices, service rules, supplied clerks.
  • Unknown 4 + Questioning 4: hesitation, partial clarification, safer late-campaign contact.

The blend matters more than any single axis.

Suggested Personality States

The Fright-Mirror

High Demon / Enemy + Threat-Shaping + Memory-Shaping.

The Violet produces the enemy Bellweather expects.

The Stern Judge

High Judge / Trial + System-Shaping + Memory-Shaping.

The Violet turns guilt into procedure.

The Soft Cage

High Guardian / Mercy + Shelter-Shaping + Body-Shaping.

The Violet reduces distress by enclosing, soothing, healing, or sedating.

The Kindly Bureau

High Utility / Service + System-Shaping + Shelter-Shaping.

The Violet provides offices, services, bills, forms, assistance, and compliance.

The Old Bargain

High Old Compact / Reciprocity + Boundary-Shaping + Memory-Shaping.

The Violet behaves like a place of old roads, offerings, taboos, and exact terms.

The Waiting Room

High Purgatory / Passage + Memory-Shaping + System-Shaping.

The Violet creates stations, lists, queues, waiting spaces, sorting systems, and dead-adjacent calm.

The Becoming God

High False God / Worship + Guardian / Mercy + System-Shaping.

The Violet begins responding like a deity because the town teaches it to receive petitions.

The Careful Listener

High Unknown / Unnamed + Questioning + Boundary-Shaping.

The Violet hesitates, clarifies, asks, and becomes less certain in harmful answers.

This is the best path toward the endgame.

GM Use

At the end of each session, ask:

  • What did the public now believe?
  • What phrase got repeated?
  • What did the town reward?
  • What did the town fear aloud?
  • What did the Violet provide?
  • Which matrix axis moved?
  • Which behavior became easier for the Violet to repeat?

Track evidence, not just numbers.

A score without evidence is dead bookkeeping.

Core Principle

The Violet becomes more dangerous when Bellweather agrees too quickly about what it is.
