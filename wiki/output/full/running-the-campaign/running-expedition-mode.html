Expedition Mode

Expedition Mode covers missions outside stable Bellweather or into unstable parts of town.

These missions may involve:

  • missing people
  • resource recovery
  • route testing
  • boundary sites
  • altered districts
  • strange infrastructure
  • false survivors
  • dream bleed
  • monsters and manifestations
  • contact with other settlements or entities

Expedition Mode is where Call of Cthulhu-style character play is strongest: investigation, danger, skills, stress, injury, and hard choices.

Away-Mission Characters

If the campaign uses civic main characters and away-mission retainers, Expedition Mode is where those retainers come into danger directly.

City Mode decides who is sent, what authority they carry, what resources they receive, and what return condition defines success.

Expedition Mode tests what that decision costs.

What Makes an Expedition Different

Inside the town center, Bellweather is reinforced by names, lamps, routines, public records, and witnesses.

Outside that stable zone, intent becomes more exposed.

A party does not merely travel through space.

It carries a signal.

That signal includes:

  • destination
  • purpose
  • fear
  • grief
  • private wishes
  • rescue desire
  • public expectation
  • route declaration
  • witness memory
  • objects carried
  • what the town believes the party is doing

The Violet may answer any part of that signal if it becomes loud enough.

Expedition Setup

Before the party leaves, define the expedition clearly.

A proper setup should include:

  • who is going
  • where they are going
  • why they are going
  • who authorized the mission
  • what route they intend to follow
  • what lanterns, maps, tokens, and records they carry
  • who witnesses the departure
  • what return gate or marker they intend to use
  • what counts as mission completion
  • what they are specifically not asking the Violet to provide

This does not need to be slow every time.

For routine missions, summarize it.

For dangerous missions, play it.

Route Declaration

Use a route declaration when destination and return matter.

Basic form:

We are [names].
We depart from [gate / location].
We travel to [destination].
Our purpose is [specific purpose].
We carry [anchors / lanterns / records].
We return to [return point].
We do not accept substitution, replacement, dream custody, unpaid tender, or unnamed addition.
We return by name.

The declaration is not a spell.

It is a shared interpretive anchor.

Expedition Scene Structure

A useful expedition can be built from five scene types.

1. Departure

Show the route office, witnesses, family members, clerks, lanterns, or public pressure.

Departure reminds the table that the mission matters to the town.

2. Route Pressure

Show drift, repetition, weather, silence, impossible signs, emotional side roads, or private memory intrusions.

Route pressure tests whether the party can hold purpose.

3. Investigation Site

The mission location contains clues, people, resources, danger, and Violet misinterpretation.

This is where normal investigative play does the most work.

4. Choice Point

Force the party to choose between safety, clarity, compassion, speed, resources, truth, or return.

The Violet should be most dangerous when the party wants several incompatible good things.

5. Return

Returning is part of the mission, not a fade-out.

Use recognition tests, witness checks, route closure, medical triage, debrief, stress recovery, and public consequence.

Common Expedition Goals

Confirm a Location

Find out whether a farm, mill, road, bridge, cemetery, or boundary marker still exists in continuity.

Recover a Person

Search for a named person using last route, last purpose, witnesses, tokens, and recognition tests.

Retrieve a Resource

Bring back food, fuel, medicine, salt, tools, records, livestock, or machinery.

Investigate a Violet Response

Study supplied infrastructure, false survivors, altered records, dream bleed, strange weather, or role-made entities.

Establish a Foothold

Mark, light, map, name, or witness an unstable place so Bellweather can use it again.

Make Contact

Find another settlement, entity, returned person, Violet-touched medium, or possible outside-world trace.

Running Clues in Expedition Mode

Do not hide the whole expedition behind one failed roll.

A failed roll can mean:

  • the clue is found late
  • the clue is misunderstood
  • the clue comes with stress
  • the party attracts attention
  • the clue exposes a Signal Leak
  • the route back becomes unstable
  • a witness is endangered
  • a resource is damaged
  • public interpretation worsens after return

The expedition should keep moving.

Failure changes what the party brings home.

Route Drift During Expeditions

Use route drift when the party’s intent weakens or conflicts.

Signs include:

  • road signs become emotionally relevant
  • a side path appears toward a remembered place
  • maps show old memories instead of terrain
  • one traveler hears a voice no one else hears
  • lantern light bends toward a private fear
  • the return phrase becomes hard to remember
  • the route begins satisfying someone’s hidden wish

Route drift should not be constant.

Use it when the party is tired, afraid, divided, grieving, or vague.

Return and Debrief

Returning to Bellweather should have procedure.

On return, ask:

  • Did the named travelers return?
  • Did anyone extra return?
  • Did anyone return missing something?
  • Do witnesses recognize them?
  • Do they recognize their own anchors?
  • Did they bring back resources, clues, or contamination?
  • What did the expedition teach the town?
  • What did it teach the Violet?

The return scene is where the expedition becomes civic consequence.

Recognition Tests

Use recognition tests for rescued persons, returned patrols, and anyone who came back under strange conditions.

Tests should check continuity, not trivia.

Examples:

  • full name
  • address
  • relationship to witnesses
  • known scar or habit
  • emotional reaction to a real person
  • recognition of a personal object
  • memory with imperfect human texture
  • ability to contradict the role they appear to fulfill

A false survivor may know the facts.

A real person carries continuity.

Expedition Consequences

After the mission, update at least one of the following:

  • resource clocks
  • public interpretation
  • faction trust
  • route stability
  • Dream Pressure
  • missing person status
  • Violet Response Matrix
  • Signal Leaks
  • town rumors
  • future mission options

Even a successful expedition should change Bellweather.

Core Principle

An expedition is not over when the party finds the thing.

It is over when Bellweather understands what came back.
