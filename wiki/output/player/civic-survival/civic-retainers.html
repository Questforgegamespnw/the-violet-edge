Retainers

Retainers are the people Bellweather sends when a civic decision needs hands, eyes, tools, courage, and risk.

They may be deputies, lantern-bearers, nurses, clerks, drivers, workers, scouts, teachers, union crews, route witnesses, farmhands, guards, mechanics, or volunteers.

They are not disposable hirelings.

They are Bellweather.

Why Retainers Matter

Retainers make the town feel larger than the player characters.

They allow the campaign to support:

  • dangerous expeditions
  • higher lethality
  • civic main characters who remain in town
  • larger public consequences
  • institutional responsibility
  • recurring NPC attachments
  • field teams with specific jobs
  • losses that hurt without ending a player’s civic role

A retainer represents trust.

Someone approved their mission. Someone equipped them. Someone waited for them to come back.

Retainer Format

Keep retainers lightweight unless they become recurring favorites.

A useful retainer can be defined with:

  • name
  • role or occupation
  • who they answer to
  • three useful skills
  • weak point
  • relationship in the city
  • item or kit
  • stress, scar, or private fear
  • loyalty or agenda
  • what losing them would cost the town

Do not overbuild every retainer.

The more mechanically complex they are, the more they stop feeling like a civic resource and start feeling like spare player characters.

Example Retainers

Deputy Mara Venn

Role: Field deputy and expedition lead Answers To: Sheriff Amos Rusk Useful Skills: Firearms, Track, Intimidate Weak Point: Pushes too hard to prove herself City Tie: Her father’s name is on the old East Road memorial Fear / Stress: Heard her dead father’s voice from East Road Kit: Revolver, route tags, police whistle, lantern Loyalty / Agenda: Bellweather first, Sheriff’s office second, missing families always Loss Cost: Sheriff’s office morale drops; Eastgate families accuse Town Hall of wasting the young Current Status: Available / shaken / missing / returned changed

Ruth Bell, Eastgate Lantern-Bearer

Role: Route witness and lantern-bearer Answers To: Deputy Captain or Eastgate route office Useful Skills: Spot Hidden, Listen, Navigate Weak Point: Panics when separated from light City Tie: Younger brother missing near Orchard Road Fear / Stress: Hearing her brother answer from the wrong direction Kit: Numbered lantern, route tags, whistle, revolver with three rounds Loyalty / Agenda: Eastgate crew first, Town Hall second Loss Cost: Eastgate patrol morale drops; future route declarations suffer Current Status: Available / assigned / wounded / witness exhausted

Jonah Pike’s Lamp Crew

Role: Lampworks repair crew Answers To: Lampworkers’ Union Useful Skills: Electrical Repair, Mechanical Repair, Heavy Labor Weak Point: Refuses unsafe orders without union consent City Tie: Keeps the public lamps burning Fear / Stress: Knows some lamps now burn without oil Kit: tools, wire, ladder truck, spare housings, union work tags Loyalty / Agenda: Worker safety and district fairness Loss Cost: Lampworks cooperation drops; poor districts accuse Town Hall of sacrifice Current Status: Available / on strike / overworked / missing crew

Nurse Clara Hobb

Role: Field nurse and sleep ward assistant Answers To: Dr. Helena Cross Useful Skills: First Aid, Medicine, Psychology Weak Point: Over-identifies with children and patients City Tie: Knows half the town’s births and deaths by name Fear / Stress: Dreams of patients waking in the wrong beds Kit: medical bag, sedatives, ration cards, hospital bracelet tags Loyalty / Agenda: Patients first, hospital authority second Loss Cost: Mercy Hospital loses trust; sleep ward care worsens Current Status: Available / exhausted / assigned to sleep ward / unfit for field work

Retainer Status

Use simple status tags.

Examples:

  • Available
  • Assigned
  • Overworked
  • Wounded
  • Missing
  • Returned
  • Returned Changed
  • Witness Exhausted
  • Refuses Duty
  • Dead
  • Disputed

Status should affect future civic actions.

A wounded retainer may reduce support. A missing retainer creates a rescue pressure. A returned changed retainer may become a clue, danger, or argument over continuity.

Retainers in City Mode

Retainers can support civic actions.

They may:

  • add +1 die if properly assigned
  • remove a penalty if they are suited to the work
  • enable an action that requires trained personnel
  • protect against a likely consequence
  • become the cost of a partial success
  • carry public meaning back into the town

Example:

The players assign Eastgate lantern-bearers to a route stabilization action. The retainers add support, but if the roll partially succeeds, one lantern-bearer returns with a route phrase they cannot stop repeating.

Retainers in Expedition Mode

Retainers may accompany player characters or serve as the player’s away-mission character if the campaign uses civic main characters.

For dangerous missions, define:

  • who sent them
  • what authority they carry
  • what resource they represent
  • what they are afraid of
  • what they know that others do not
  • what happens if they fail to return

A retainer on expedition should have at least one thing they are useful for and one reason they are vulnerable.

Command Has Consequences

If a retainer dies, vanishes, returns changed, or causes harm, the consequences should reach the main character, institution, or faction that sent them.

Ask:

  • Who gave the order?
  • Was the mission necessary?
  • Were the risks understood?
  • Were the retainers properly equipped?
  • What did the town gain?
  • What did the town lose?
  • Who now blames the decision-maker?
  • What public meaning does the loss create?
  • What did the Violet learn from the mission?

This keeps retainers from becoming spare lives.

A retainer is not a spare life.

A retainer is someone Bellweather trusted.

Advancement and Attachment

If a retainer survives multiple missions, let them become more specific.

They may gain:

  • a new skill
  • a fear
  • a scar
  • a stronger loyalty
  • a grudge
  • a useful contact
  • a public reputation
  • a Signal Leak
  • a relationship with a player character
  • a role in a future civic action

Do not level retainers like full player characters unless the table wants that.

Let survival make them meaningful.

What Can Go Wrong

Retainer systems can become cruel if the town starts using people as expendable tools.

Watch for:

  • poor districts always supplying dangerous labor
  • volunteers becoming coerced
  • retainers sent without proper gear
  • families not told the truth
  • missing retainers declared dead too early
  • returned retainers treated as proof instead of people
  • workers punished for refusing unsafe orders
  • the Violet learning that roles matter more than personhood

These are not reasons to avoid retainers.

They are reasons to make retainer use morally visible.

Core Principle

Retainers are how Bellweather reaches beyond itself.

Every person sent out carries the town with them.
